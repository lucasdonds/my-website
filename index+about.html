About Me

Hello and welcome to my website! I am a Mechanical Engineering student at the University of Waterloo going into my second year of studies. I am interested in design, machines, programming, and above all creating! I am a person who likes to think outside of the box in order to find new and creative solutions to challenging problems. When working in groups, I find I often take the team lead and distribute work, trying to play to each individual members strengths, and ensuring everybody on the team is working on something interesting and challenging. However, I am not a controlling lead, I am actually super chill.

Work Experience

So far I have two internships under my belt. For the first one, I was lucky to have a great placement at Skyjack - a subsidiary of Linamar - where I worked as a part of their large manufacturing engineering team! My main responsibilities during my 4 month term in there included managing and updating the plant layouts in order to improve flow and use of labour. I did this by taking lean manufacturing concepts into consideration, such as decreasing cycle time and making picking from warehousing more efficient. Additionally, I was a part of the team responsible for implementing new robotic equipment into the assembly line, which allowed for better use and reduction of labour. Overall, I thoroughly enjoyed my time at Skyjack and am very grateful for the opportunity they offered me, I learnt a lot.

My second placement was at WATiMake - an engineering clinic on campus focused on rapid prototyping. The clinic is open to all students on campus, but is focused on mechanical and mechatronics engineering. There, my responsibilities included maintaining and repairing the machines, - including 8 FDM and 2 SLA 3D printers, a laser cutter, vinyl cutter, and CNC machine - advising and helping students on their own projects, and working on a term project with my coworker. The term project was to make an active suspension system to demonstrate certain control systems concepts such as frequency analysis and harmonic damper. The project included learning concepts such as establishing serial connections between devices (Arduino and MATLAB) in order to collect data from the system, analysing the data, and designing the engageable active suspension components using a brushless dc motor.
